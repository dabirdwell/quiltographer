Notes Before You Begin 
-Read through all of the instructions before beginning. 
-All of the seam allowances are 1/4” unless otherwise noted. 
-Press seam allowances open unless otherwise noted. 
-Width of fabric (WOF) is equal to at least 42” wide. 
-Length of fabric has been abbreviated to LOF. 
-Right sides together has been abbreviated to RST. 
-Remember to measure twice and cut once!

4tFQ : Pin two 1-7/8” Fabric B squares in opposite corners of a 
3-1/4” Fabric I Squares, RST. The squares will overlap slightly in the 
center of the larger square. Using a ruler, draw a diagonal line 
through the squares. 
4tFQ : Sew a scant 1/4” seam on each side of the diagonal 
line. Set the seam by pressing it flat. Cut along the diagonal line, 
creating two identical units. 
4tFQ : Press the fabric toward the smaller triangles. Repeat with the 
second unit. 
4tFQ : Place one 1-7/8” Fabric B square at the corner of sewn block. 
Unit. Draw diagonal line. Sew a scant 1/4” seam on each side of the 
diagonal line. Set seam by pressing. Cut along the diagonal line, 
creating two identical units. 
4tFQ : Press the seam toward smaller triangle. Trim off small dog ears left 
by the seam allowance. Trim to 1-1/2” x 2-1/2” if necessary. 
4tFQ : Repeat with all remaining 1-7/8” Fabric B squares and 3-1/4” 
Fabric I squares to make a total of twenty four B/I flying geese units.

1-7/8” Fabric D, E, F, and G squares to make the following:
