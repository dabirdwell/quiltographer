Step 1: Gather 
two Fabric A rectangles 
two Fabric A squares 
two Fabric B rectangles 
two Fabric B squares 
Note: Fabric A will now be referred to as the Background Fabric, and Fabrics B-O will 
be referred to as the Print Fabrics. 
From Fabric A, cut: 
twenty-three  3-1/2” x WOF strips. Subcut: 
 ninety 3-1/2” x 7” rectangles 
 ninety 3-1/2” squares. 
Mark a diagonal on the back side of each square. 
From each of Fabrics B-L, cut: 
two 3-1/2” x WOF strips. Subcut: 
 six 3-1/2” x 7” rectangles six 3-1/2” squares. 
Mark a diagonal on the back side of each square. 
From each of Fabrics M-O, cut: 
three 3-1/2” x WOF strips. Subcut: 
 eight 3-1/2” x 7” rectangles eight 3-1/2” squares. 
Mark a diagonal on the back side of each square. 
From the Binding Fabric, cut: 
seven 2-1/2” x WOF strips

-Read through all of the instructions before beginning.
-All of the seam allowances are 1/4” unless otherwise noted.
-Press seam allowances open unless otherwise noted. 
-Width of fabric (WOF) is equal to at least 42” wide.
-Length of fabric has been abbreviated to LOF.
-Right sides together has been abbreviated to RST.
-Remember to measure twice and cut once!

Step 3: Place a Print square at the top edge of a 
Background rectangle, right sides together. Note 
the orientation of the marked diagonal line and 
how it is the mirror image of the marked line in 
Step 2. Sew on the marked line, then trim away 
the excess fabric, leaving a 1/4” seam allowance. 
Press. Repeat to make a second unit like this. 
Step 4: Arrange the units into two rows of two. Note the fabric placement and 
orientation in the Block Assembly Diagram. 
Step 5: Sew the units together to form the rows. Press the seams to the left in 
the top row and to the right in the bottom row. 
Step 6: Sew the rows together, nesting the seams and pressing the seams to 
one side to form the block. 
The Block should measure 6-1/2” x 13-1/2”. Repeat to 
make: three Blocks from each of Fabrics B-L four Blocks 
from each of Fabrics M-O. 
Step 7: Arrange the blocks into five rows 
of nine Blocks. Note the fabric placement 
and block orientation in the Quilt 
Assembly Diagram. 
Step 8: Sew the blocks together to form 
rows. Press the rows to the left in the odd 
numbered rows and to the right in the 
even numbered rows. 
Step 9: Sew the rows together to form 
the quilt center. Press the row seams 
Your quilt top is complete! Baste, 
quilt, bind and enjoy!
