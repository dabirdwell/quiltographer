# Paper Piecing Reference for Quiltographer

## Technique Overview

**Foundation Paper Piecing (FPP)** is a quilting technique where fabric pieces are sewn onto a paper template in a specific numbered sequence. The paper provides precision—complex designs become achievable through simple straight-line sewing.

### Key Characteristics
- All sewing is straight lines (no curved piecing)
- Numbered sequence guides assembly order
- Paper templates ensure precision regardless of skill level
- Enables photo-realistic and highly detailed designs
- Y-seams and tricky angles can be avoided through good pattern design

## Reference Site: Pride & Joy Quilting

**URL**: https://prideandjoyquilting.com/

### Why This Reference Matters

Pride & Joy Quilting creates museum-quality portrait quilts using FPP, demonstrating the technique's upper bounds. Their patterns show what's achievable:

**Example: "Girl with A Pearl Earring" Pattern**
- **Size**: 60" × 60"
- **Complexity**: 76 individual FPP units
- **Fabric shapes**: 563 total pieces
- **Colors**: 15 fabrics
- **Pattern pages**: 86 (plus 35 pages of instructions)
- **Key feature**: 100% straight-line sewing, zero Y-seams

### Pattern Structure (FPP Standard)
1. **Unit pages** - Individual paper piecing templates with numbered sections
2. **Assembly diagrams** - How units connect into larger blocks
3. **Color charts** - Fabric recommendations per numbered section
4. **Sequence blocking** - Order for joining completed units
5. **Blank templates** - For custom color planning

### Technique-Specific Considerations
- Sections must be numbered in sewable order
- Each section needs add seam allowances
- Paper removal after sewing (design for tearability)
- Mirror image awareness (sew on reverse of printed side)
