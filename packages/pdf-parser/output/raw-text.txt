Notes Before You Begin

From Fabric C, cut:

 ten 2” x 18-1/2” top/bottom outer block borders
From Fabric D, cut:

 forty 4” squares for the HSTs
nine 2” x WOF strips. Subcut:

one 4” x WOF strip. Subcut:
 ten 4” squares for the HSTs
From each of Fabrics F and I, cut:
one 4” x WOF strip. Subcut:
 six 4” squares for the HSTs
From Fabric H, cut:
one 4” x WOF strip. Subcut:

Cutting Instructions
Step 1: Pair a 4” Fabric D square with a 4” Fabric E square, RST. Mark a diagonal line 
on the wrong side of one square. Sew 1/4” away from both sides of the marked line. 
Cut on the marked line, creating two half-square triangles (HSTs). Press toward the 
darker fabric. Trim to 3-1/2” square. Repeat to make twenty D/E HSTs.
Assemble the Half-Square Triangles (HSTs)
Step 2: Repeat the process in Step 1 to make:
twelve D/F HSTstwenty D/G HSTssixteen D/H HSTstwelve D/I HSTs

page 4
Assemble the Blocks
Step 3: Gather:
three D/G HSTs
three D/H HSTs
four D/E HSTs
three D/F HSTs
three D/I HSTs
Arrange the units into four rows of four. Note the fabric placement 
and block orientation in the Block Assembly Diagram. 
Step 4: Sew the HSTs together to form the rows. Press the seams in opposite directions every 
other row. 
Step 5: Sew the rows together, nesting the seams and pressing the seams to one side to form 
the block center, measuring 12-1/2” square. 
Step 6: Sew the Fabric D side inner block borders to the sides of the block center. Press 
toward the borders. Sew the Fabric D top and bottom inner block borders to the block 
center. Press toward the borders.
Step 7: Sew the Fabric C side outer block borders to the sides of the block center. Press 
toward the borders. Sew the Fabric C top and bottom outer block borders to the block 
center. Press toward the borders.
The Block should measure 18-1/2” square. Make four Blocks like this.
Step 8: Repeat the process in Steps 4-7, this time with:
four D/E HSTs
eight D/G HSTs
four D/H HSTs
Make one Block like this. 
Step 9: Sew the Fabric B side outer borders to the sides of the Fabric A squares. Press  
toward the borders. Sew the Fabric B top and bottom outer borders to the Fabric A squares. 
Press toward the borders. The resulting block should measure 18-1/2” square. 

page 5
Step 10: Arrange the blocks into three rows of three blocks. Note the fabric placement and 
block orientation in the Quilt Assembly Diagram. 
Step 11: Sew the blocks together to form rows. Press the rows to the left in the odd numbered 
rows and to the right in the even numbered rows. 
Step 12: Sew the rows together to form the quilt center. Press the row seams open.
Your quilt top is complete. Baste, quilt, bind and enjoy!
